The character was co-created by writer and editor Stan Lee, developed by scripter Larry Leiber, and designed by artist Don Heck and Jack K irby. The charcter made his first appearancein Tales of Suspence #39 (cover dated March 1963) and recieved his own title in Iron Man.

Publication history

Premiere

Following the success of the Fantastic Four in 1961 and the subsequent revival of superhero comic books, Marvel set about creating new superhero characters. During this time, Stan Lee developed the initial concept for Iron Man.

Solo Series

Lee was interested in making an unlikeable character likeable, saying that he "thought it would be fun to take the kind of character that nobody would like, none of [Marvel's] readers would like, and shove him down their throats and make them like him".

Fictional character biography

Tony Stark inherited his family's business, Stark Industries, after his parents were killed in a car crash.[69] Developing equipment for the U.S. military, he travels to a war zone to conduct a weapons test when he triggers a booby trap.
